About Me

I’m Caner Çoban, a Computational Linguistics graduate with a Master’s from Eberhard Karls University in Tübingen, Germany. With a solid foundation in programming, natural language processing, and artificial intelligence, I am driven to tackle challenges at the intersection of language and technology. My academic and hands-on project experience spans advanced AI techniques, data science, and machine learning applications, including projects like sarcasm detection using transformer models and the creation of a finite-state morphological analyzer for Gagauz Turkish.

Beyond technical skills in Python, Java, SQL, and machine learning frameworks like PyTorch and TensorFlow, I bring a strong analytical mindset and a collaborative spirit. Whether it's optimizing text embedding techniques or developing novel frameworks to quantify dialectal features, I thrive in projects that allow me to push boundaries and apply innovative solutions..

In addition to my technical pursuits, I enjoy cycling, cinema, and the strategic challenges of gaming. I am also passionate about environmental volunteering and am an enthusiast of Tolkien's Middle-earth, with a particular interest in language and storytelling.
